What is this site?
${site['site_name']} is a crowd-sourced database of market board information for ${site['game_server']}, freely shared under the belief that knowing market trends will help both crafters and gatherers profit, without leading to a Prisoner's Dilemma environment.
If it's freely shared, why the authentication?
The first reason is to prevent data-mining by those who would try to use in-game crowd-sourced data for real-world profit. The second is to prevent vandalism. Hand-in-hand with the second is the appeal of knowing you're part of an elite club.
So how does this system work?
Simple: pick an item, type a price, and hit update!

If the item you want to track doesn't exist, just type its name anyway (be sure to spell it right, though capitalisation doesn't matter) and when you submit a price, it'll be created; there's a field at the top of the page specifically for this.

To add an HQ item variant, just put 'HQ' at the end of its name.

If you made a mistake when entering a price, just click on the item page to delete it; if it's the only price, the item will be removed, too. You can only delete prices entered recently, to preserve historical integrity.

for convenience, anywhere you see a timestamp attached to an item (the grey '8 hours ago' text), you can click on it to expose an update prompt.
What are the key things to keep in mind?
  • Prices should reflect the effective market-floor of an item, ignoring obvious outliers
    • Outliers are things like items listed at 5g when the market regularly moves at 500g or enormous stacks when people normally only buy singles
    • As a general rule of thumb, use the price you would have to pay if you were to buy the item in question in reasonable quantities
  • Use this system to track any items you're interested in, but farmable commodities are most preferred, and tracking those will help everyone weather (or profit from) spikes
  • While it would be interesting to know the prices of high-value items, like housing pieces, you are under no obligation to publish anything that you would prefer to keep to yourself, though others may contribute it independently
  • Please be respectful of market pricing: don't be the person who undercuts a lucrative operation by 50%, especially if you learned of it through this site
How does authorisation work?
When you request access, you must provide your character's name on ${site['game_server']}. This will create a profile for you with its status set to "unconfirmed", and you will get a unique login code, which you should record for later use.

At this point, you must message a moderator in-game to be given access; this is to ensure that you are the person who requested access, since the names must match.

At any point, your profile may be banned if you are found to be acting against the spirit of this database.

What privacy is provided?
Your character name is stored as your identification within the server, and it is tied to all price-data you submit.

The password you provide is hashed with a unique-per-user salt, which should make it extremely hard to crack, and it is connected only to your in-game name, not an e-mail address or account ID. However, no guarantees are made about the security of this information.

Cookies are used solely to retain your logged-in status for convenience. Your IP may be recorded as part of routine logging, but it is not tied to any other identifying data, beyond fuzzy correlation of access-time with price-data.

This is not a guarantee or commitment to privacy, but the spirit with which the system was written is one of analysing market trends purely for the sake of fascination, not to exploit its users.
